Problems Faced in Agriculture

The agricultural sector faces numerous challenges that impact productivity, profitability, and sustainability.

Market Access & Pricing

Farmers struggle with limited market access, multiple intermediaries, and opaque pricing mechanisms that reduce their bargaining power and potential earnings.

Supply Chain & Logistics

Post-harvest losses occur due to inadequate transportation, long transit times, and lack of cold storage facilities, especially in remote villages.

Awareness & Education

Limited access to up-to-date information on modern farming techniques, disease management, and government schemes affects productivity.

Financial Constraints

Farmers face barriers to accessing formal credit, complex insurance processes, and lack reliable tools for financial planning and forecasting.

Weather Dependency

Agriculture is highly susceptible to unpredictable weather patterns, including erratic monsoons, droughts, and extreme temperatures.

Input Quality & Price

Farmers struggle with unreliable quality and inconsistent pricing of inputs like seeds, fertilizers, and pesticides, often dealing with counterfeit products.

Challenges for Retailers & Wholesalers

Supply & Transparency

Retailers and wholesalers face inconsistent quality and unreliable supply of produce, making inventory management difficult. Lack of robust traceability systems makes it impossible to verify origin and quality certifications.

Pricing & Storage

Price disputes between buyers and sellers are common due to lack of real-time data. Inefficient storage solutions and poor inventory management lead to significant wastage.
